Osmany Bonet

(Havana, 1984) Photographer and visual artist. Currently teaches photography workshops. Graduated in 2013 from the Creative Photography School of Havana, where he later taught various subjects and eventually became its Technical Deputy Director.

Through photography, he explores the connections between nature and people, seeking elements that describe or reflect the relationship that develops between them. He works on and leads several national-level photography projects, teaches workshops and lectures on photography, and has participated in numerous photographic exhibitions both in Cuba and abroad. He serves as a jury member for the international architectural photography competition “Artistic Lens,” held annually in Cuba.

He has been part of the exhibitions “Miradas Compartidas” at the Palacio de Lombillo and “Matahambre” at the University of the Arts, both part of the 15th Havana Biennial. In 2024, together with other artists, he won the Punto Naranja Artist Residency with the project “Cosecha de Luz”, awarded by the Wilfredo Lam Contemporary Art Center.

Studio Photography

Where precision meets creativity, the studio becomes a controlled space to sculpt light, form, and expression. Osmany captures every detail with intention, transforming concepts into polished visual stories that reflect a refined artistic language.

Street Photography

Spontaneity and rhythm unfold in the streets. From fleeting moments to raw emotion, Osmany’s street photography invites viewers into unfiltered urban narratives shaped by movement, contrast, and the unpredictable poetry of public life.

Nature, Landscape & Travel

A journey through landscapes becomes a search for meaning. Osmany frames the harmony and tension between humans and the environment, translating textures, vastness, and silence into contemplative visual experiences.

Portrait

Each portrait becomes a quiet collaboration between artist and subject. Osmany draws out intimate expressions and layered personalities, crafting images that resonate with emotional depth and honest connection.

Food

More than flavor—food is culture, ritual, and memory. Osmany’s food photography combines composition and atmosphere to tell sensory stories that go beyond the plate and into tradition, place, and community.

Events

Every event holds its own energy. From subtle gestures to grand moments, Osmany’s lens captures the rhythm of gatherings with discretion and sensitivity, preserving experiences as layered visual narratives.
